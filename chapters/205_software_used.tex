Integrating \textbf{deep reinforcement learning} with Unity forces us to confront the \textbf{``Two-Language Problem.''} The stack physically separates the high-speed simulation in \textbf{C\#} from the vectorized tensor operations in \textbf{Python}. Unity's C\# backend rigorously computes spatial collision physics and snare kinematics at hundreds of frames per second. Concurrently, Python manages the learning algorithm, pushing states through \textbf{PyTorch} to update the neural policy. Bridging these two environments requires flawless \textbf{inter-process communication (IPC)}. We rely on \textbf{gRPC} protocols and \textbf{shared memory buffers} to maintain a high-frequency telemetry pipeline. Python fires motor actions across the bridge, while Unity instantly replies with rendered pixel arrays and reward scalars. Managing this fragile socket connection without desynchronizing the RL loop proved to be one of the project's most severe engineering bottlenecks.

This diverse stack of technologies was absolutely crucial for bringing the project to life. At the core of our AI brains, \textbf{PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization)} drove the policy updates, while a \textbf{CNN} handled visual feature extraction and an \textbf{LSTM} provided essential temporal memory. \textbf{CUDA} was the powerhouse behind these heavy training workloads, accelerating the millions of iterations our agents needed. For presenting our findings, \textbf{Reveal.js} allowed us to build dynamic, browser-based slides, while \textbf{PDF.js} seamlessly integrated our final report viewer directly into the web app. Finally, having \textbf{Arch Linux} for our flexible server environments and \textbf{Windows 11} as the primary workstation gave us the best of both worlds for development and testing.
