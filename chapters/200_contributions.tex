\section{Introduction}
In this chapter we'll document our main technical contribution: a \textbf{two-phase} Unity simulation that uses deep reinforcement learning for \textbf{endoscope navigation} and \textbf{snare capture}.
Robotic or assisted endoscopy really needs two main capabilities: robust navigation through some very constrained anatomical spaces, and precise manipulation when a therapeutic action (like sniping a polyp) is required. Did you know that the colon has a total internal surface area of roughly 2,000 cm\textsuperscript{2}? That's about the size of a small desk crammed into your belly! Navigating it is no joke. To make the learning process tractable for our AI, we split the work into two distinct phases: Phase~1 (navigation) and Phase~2 (snare capture). Separating these tasks simplifies training, dramatically reduces the exploration burden, and enables us to evaluate each capability clearly.

\textbf{What this project is - and what it is not:}
Let's be clear upfront. This project features \textbf{vision-only} agents that learn purely from camera images. We are not using any explicit polyp detector or segmentation model. Moreover, this is a simulated environment with simplified physics and geometric polyp models, meaning it is perfect for algorithmic development, but it's not a clinical substitute ready to be tested on humans tomorrow!

In this chapter, we'll dive into how we built this virtual world, the action interfaces for our agents, how we shaped their rewards to prevent them from crashing, and the neural network brains powering the whole operation.
