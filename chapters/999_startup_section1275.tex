\section{Project Idea \& Proposed Solution}
\textbf{Field of Activity:} Healthcare Technology, Artificial Intelligence, Medical Software.

\textbf{Overview:}
The project aims to develop a deep reinforcement learning-based solution for intelligent polypectomy, assisting in the navigation of endoscopes and the precise capture of polyps. By utilizing a two-phase vision-only agent, the system can learn purely from camera images without requiring explicit physical sensors, paving the way for advanced assisted endoscopy.

\section{Value Proposition}
The proposed solution offers value through the following aspects:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Novelty:} Introducing cutting-edge deep reinforcement learning into endoscopic navigation and manipulation.
    \item \textbf{Performance:} Achieving high precision in automated snare capture within constrained anatomical spaces.
    \item \textbf{Risk Reduction:} Minimizing human error during surgical interventions and reducing the likelihood of incomplete resections.
    \item \textbf{Usability \& Cost Reduction:} Operating seamlessly from vision-only data, reducing the need for expensive additional sensory hardware.
\end{itemize}

\section{Work Team \& Organization}
The team is composed of data science and artificial intelligence specialists. The responsibilities are distributed optimally between environmental modeling (Unity), deep reinforcement learning training (PPO, LSTM), and systems integration. Regular communication and agile iterations ensure that both the navigation and resection phases are developed concurrently.

\section{Project Goals \& Timeline}
The primary innovative goal is to secure a patent for the vision-based RL navigation system. The projected timeline involves the following milestones:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Researching patent databases and gathering state-of-the-art information.
    \item Beginning laboratory tests and software development to prepare the prototype.
    \item Testing the prototype in simulated environments (Unity).
    \item Registering the patent to obtain industrial protection.
    \item Following up on the patent examination process and addressing reviewers' feedback.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Market Analysis \& Business Model}
\subsection{Market Segment}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Potential Market:} Hospitals, specialized clinics, and global medical equipment manufacturers.
    \item \textbf{Target Market:} Endoscopic surgery departments seeking AI-assisted tools to improve procedure safety and efficiency.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Competition Analysis}
Competitors include traditional medical device companies and emerging med-tech startups focusing on surgical robotics. Our competitive advantage lies in our software-first, vision-only approach, which is adaptable, scalable, and bypasses the need for complex multi-sensor calibration.

\subsection{Financial Overview}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Costs \& Expenses:} Software licensing, high-performance computing hardware (GPUs) for model training, and intellectual property registration fees.
    \item \textbf{Revenue Streams:} Licensing the AI software to medical device manufacturers, offering software-as-a-service (SaaS) updates, and potential direct sales to healthcare providers.
\end{itemize}

\section{Experimental Prototype}
The experimental prototype consists of a simulated Unity environment featuring simplified geometric colon models and a virtual endoscope. It successfully demonstrates the two-phase RL approach (Navigation and Resection). This prototype serves as the foundational proof-of-concept for the startup, showcasing the feasibility of AI-driven therapeutic actions.
