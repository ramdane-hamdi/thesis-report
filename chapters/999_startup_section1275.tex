\section{Project Idea (Proposed Solution)}

\textbf{Field of Activity:} Healthcare Technology, Artificial Intelligence, and Medical Software.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{How did the idea begin and how did it develop?} \\
The concept originated from the need to reduce human error and minimize the high dependence on manual expertise during colonoscopies. Initially focused on simply detecting polyps (CADe), the idea evolved into a more ambitious active intervention approach. By leveraging cutting-edge deep reinforcement learning, we shifted towards developing a system that can actively assist in both the navigation of the endoscope and the precise capture (resection) of polyps.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{What are we going to do?} \\
We are developing a deep reinforcement learning-based solution for intelligent polypectomy. The system uses a two-phase vision-only agent to assist in endoscopic navigation and the automated snare capture of polyps without requiring expensive multi-sensor calibration.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{How will that be done?} \\
The solution will be developed by training an RL agent (utilizing algorithms such as PPO and LSTM) within a simulated Unity environment that mimics the colon's geometry. The agent will learn purely from visual inputs (camera images) to perform the therapeutic actions, simulating a software-first approach.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Who will accomplish that?} \\
The project will be accomplished by our dedicated team of SDIA students, Hamdi Ramdane and Fellah Haithem, under the main supervision of Pr. Chaker Mezioud. The team's responsibilities cover environmental modeling, AI model training, and system integration.

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Where will it be accomplished?} \\
The initial software development, simulation, and laboratory testing will be conducted at our university research facilities. Upon successful prototyping and patent registration, the system will be targeted for clinical validation and deployment in partner hospitals and specialized gastroenterology clinics.

\section{Value Proposition}
The proposed solution offers value through the following aspects:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Novelty:} Satisfying an entirely new need by introducing cutting-edge deep reinforcement learning into active endoscopic navigation and manipulation, unlike traditional passive systems.
    \item \textbf{Performance:} Exceeding expectations by achieving high precision in automated snare capture within constrained anatomical spaces.
    \item \textbf{Customization:} Offering a highly flexible, software-first approach that can be adapted and integrated into various existing endoscopic hardware setups based on specific hospital needs.
    \item \textbf{Getting the Job Done:} Directly assisting the surgeon in accomplishing the critical and delicate task of accurately resecting polyps.
    \item \textbf{Design:} Making the system's design compatible with the surgeon's workflow by operating seamlessly from vision-only data, avoiding the clutter of extra physical sensors.
    \item \textbf{Cost Reduction:} Helping healthcare providers reduce costs by eliminating the need for expensive multi-sensor calibration or specialized robotic hardware.
    \item \textbf{Risk Reduction:} Minimizing human error and surgeon fatigue during interventions, effectively reducing the probability of incomplete resections and associated patient risks.
    \item \textbf{Accessibility:} Making advanced AI-assisted therapeutic capabilities accessible to standard gastroenterology clinics that previously could not afford complex surgical robotics.
    \item \textbf{Usability \& Convenience:} Providing a highly intuitive system that simplifies complex navigation and resection tasks, making them exceptionally easy and seamless to perform.
\end{itemize}

\section{Market Analysis \& Business Model}
\subsection{Market Size and Potential}
The global artificial intelligence in endoscopy market is experiencing rapid expansion, driven by the increasing demand for minimally invasive diagnostic and therapeutic procedures. As of 2026, the market size is estimated at approximately \$2.3 billion, and it is projected to grow at a strong Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of roughly 24\%, reaching figures between \$14 billion to over \$20 billion by the mid-2030s. 
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Potential Market:} Hospitals, specialized gastroenterology clinics, and global medical equipment manufacturers.
    \item \textbf{Target Market:} Endoscopic surgery departments seeking advanced AI-assisted tools to improve procedure safety, increase throughput, and reduce human error.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Competition Analysis}
The competitive landscape comprises traditional medical device giants and emerging med-tech startups focusing on computer vision and surgical robotics. Key competitors include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Medtronic (GI Genius):} A traditional medical device giant offering a prominent computer-aided detection (CADe) module.
    \item \textbf{Iterative Health:} An emerging med-tech startup focusing on applying computer vision for precision gastroenterology.
    \item \textbf{Odin Vision:} An innovative startup specializing in AI-driven solutions for the detection and diagnosis (CADx) of polyps.
\end{itemize}
Most of these existing solutions focus primarily on passive computer-aided detection (CADe) or diagnosis (CADx) of polyps. 

Our core competitive advantage lies in advancing from passive detection to \textbf{active intervention}. Our software-first, vision-only approach for intelligent polypectomy navigation and resection is highly adaptable, scalable, and bypasses the need for expensive, complex multi-sensor calibration often required by competitors. By relying solely on the endoscope's camera feed, Reinova significantly reduces integration friction for hospitals.

\subsection{Financial Overview and Funding Requirements}
Developing a medical-grade AI device involves considerable costs, primarily driven by rigorous clinical validation, high-performance computing infrastructure, and regulatory compliance. Tailoring the project for the Algerian startup ecosystem (e.g., aiming for funding via the Algerian Startup Fund - ASF), we estimate a realistic initial seed capital requirement of approximately \textbf{11,000,000 DZD to 19,000,000 DZD} to achieve MVP clinical validation and market entry.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Regulatory Compliance \& Clinical Validation (4,000,000 DZD - 7,000,000 DZD):} These local figures are significantly more affordable than international multi-million dollar FDA/CE trials. They cover the hiring of a local consultant to establish a Quality Management System (QMS) (estimated at 1M - 2M DZD) and the administrative, ethical, and insurance costs to run initial clinical trials in partnership with local Algerian University Hospitals (CHUs) (estimated at 3M - 5M DZD).
    \item \textbf{Software Development \& Infrastructure (5,000,000 DZD - 8,000,000 DZD):} Calculated meticulously based on local and converted international costs:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textit{Infrastructure:} Renting high-performance cloud GPUs (e.g., AWS/GCP) for training the complex RL models costs approximately \$10,000 to \$15,000 USD/year (converting to roughly 1.35M - 2M DZD).
        \item \textit{Salaries:} Hiring 2 to 3 specialized local AI engineers at a competitive Algerian startup salary of 120,000 to 150,000 DZD per month for 12 months totals roughly 3.5M - 5.4M DZD.
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Intellectual Property \& Operations (2,000,000 DZD - 4,000,000 DZD):} This accounts for local INAPI patent registration alongside international PCT filing (via WIPO) estimating at \$3,000 to \$5,000 USD (roughly 400k - 675k DZD). The remainder covers legal fees for startup incorporation and hospital contracts (500k - 1M DZD), as well as operational logistics and hardware needed to deploy pilot programs (1M - 2M DZD).
    \item \textbf{Revenue Streams:} In the post-approval phase, we anticipate revenue through licensing the AI software directly to regional healthcare networks (B2B) and offering Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) subscriptions for regular model updates and maintenance.
\end{itemize}

\section{Experimental Prototype}
The experimental prototype consists of a simulated Unity environment featuring simplified geometric colon models and a virtual endoscope. It successfully demonstrates the two-phase RL approach (Navigation and Resection). This prototype serves as the foundational proof-of-concept for the startup, showcasing the feasibility of AI-driven therapeutic actions.
